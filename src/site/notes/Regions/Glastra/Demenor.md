# Demenor

## The Ancient Empire - The Republic of the Circles

For almost ten thousand years, Demenor lived under the shadow of a single ruler: [[NPC/Koptrakhos\|Koptrakhos]], the _Eternal Emperor_. His figure, more myth than man in the last millennia, marked every structure of the country. He was neither a tireless conqueror nor a capricious despot. He ruled with distance. With calculation. With a coolness that seemed to need neither love nor approval, but only continuity.

His main concern was not expansion, but permanence.
[[NPC/Koptrakhos\|Koptrakhos]] never attempted to extend his rule beyond the borders of Demenor. Some say that he could not take his curse to other lands; others say that he simply did not wish to leave the territory where it protected him. Neighbouring nations, for their part, also showed little interest in invading a land marked by imperial spells and curses. After the first conflicts and tensions, Demenor fell into a practical isolation that, far from damaging it, consolidated its identity. The Empire could exist on its own.

## The Empire and Magic

The centralisation of magic was the great structural legacy of [[NPC/Koptrakhos\|Koptrakhos]]. All practitioners of the Art were obliged to train and reside in the Circles or the curse that covered the land would cause them bad luck that would increase exponentially until they died. The Circles were arcane-administrative institutions scattered throughout the land. There, knowledge was accumulated, advances were recorded, theory was discussed. With the wizards concentrated and subject to a binding oath to the Emperor, the magical evolution of Demenor was dizzying. What elsewhere was scattered tradition became a systematic corpus there.

Magic ceased to be a privilege or a mystery and became a tool.

Magicians travelled the imperial roads, attending to requests from villages and cities: they reinforced bridges, purified water, diagnosed strange illnesses, erected barriers against plagues or storms. Always accompanied by [[Regulators\|Regulators]], soldiers trained not only in combat, but in arcane theory. Regulators knew how to counter spells, how to interrupt rituals, how to detect deviance. At times, the line between Wizard and Regulator was tenuous; but the Regulator's loyalty to the Empire was above any affinity with the Craft.

The ranks of the Circles were nurtured in many ways. Some joined by vocation or natural talent. Others did so out of necessity, seeing magical training as a means of survival against the curse. Some were sent as punishment, turned into arcane servants after crimes or political intrigue. And there were more than a few families who gave their children to the Circles, seeking to ensure shelter, education and purpose.


### The Superchery or Folk Magic

However, while the institutional magic flourished, another current persisted in the domestic shadows: the _Superchery_ or _Folk Magic_.

Small traditions passed down from generation to generation. Wheat wreaths hung on trees to secure crops. Lead plates buried with dark wishes written on them. Murmurs to unseen spirits. Discreet gestures before crossing a threshold. No one knew if they worked. But no one stopped practising them altogether. They were remnants of a past that predated the Empire. And they survived, even under the watchful eye of the Circles.

## The Empire and other regions

In foreign policy, Demenor was a surprisingly stable country. The Church of Aureon attempted a crusade in the 3rd century of the Golden Calendar, but its armies never penetrated deeply. Two invasions from Rhanok, in the 23rd and 34th Centuries, forced the Emperor himself onto the battlefield. Both failed. Apart from these episodes, isolation was the norm.

The people did not live in misery. The wizards, though bound by oath, were not exploited. Demenor thrived in its strange balance: severe control, but real efficiency.

## The End of the Immortal Emperor

Everything changed just over three hundred years ago.

During the [[Lore/Wave of Darkness/Wave of Darkness\|Wave of Darkness]] Demenor tried to stay out of it. But the demonic hordes recognised neither borders nor neutrality. For the first time in millennia, the Empire was overrun on multiple fronts. Cities filled with displaced people. Roads burned. Circles became makeshift fortresses.

And in the midst of the chaos, the unthinkable happened.

A hungry girl sneaked into the Imperial Palace through the kitchens. What happened next is the stuff of legend. When it was over, Koptrakhos had ceased to exist. And with him, the curse on Demenor.

The succession was a disaster.

No one had foreseen the demise of a ruler who had been on the throne for almost ten thousand years. There were no heirs. There was no protocol. The nobility reorganised into a new imperial caste after violent disputes, even with demons ravaging entire regions. The emperors and regents that followed lacked the weight, fear or stability of their predecessor. For decades, Demenor oscillated between intrigues, internal coups and weakened authority.

## Birth of the Republic of Demenor

Thirty-seven years ago, the Mages of the Circles broke the last vestiges of the oath that bound them. United with the people and supported by the Regulators, they drove out the Imperial nobility. The former rulers were loaded onto three ships known as _The Three Sorrows of Lypy_ and sent to Serra, where their fate was no longer a Demenorian affair.

Since then, Demenor has been a republic.

A complex, fragile and constantly tense republic.

A Chancellor is elected by a Senate composed of regional representatives, Circle delegates and Regulator commanders. Decisions are rarely unanimous. Legislatures contradict each other. Alliances shift. The memory of ten thousand years of centralisation weighs on every debate.

And yet borders have opened. Trade flows. Demenor is active in the politics of Glastra and all of Oreka. His wizards no longer serve an immortal emperor, but a state still learning to govern itself.

The Old Empire no longer exists.

But its shadow still defines the shape of the Republic.
