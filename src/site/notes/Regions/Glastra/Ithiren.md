# Ithiren

- **Capital**: [[Ithiranas\|Ithiranas]]
- **Population**: Ithirenenil Elves (majority), Humans, Dwarves, Halfling, Dragonborn, other minorities.

Ithiren is the land of the *Ithirenenil*, the so-called _Elves of the Moons_. Situated on the eastern edge of Glastra, their kingdom is known throughout the continent as **the Kingdom of Silver**, both for the vast reserves of this metal that lie beneath its soil and for the artistic finesse with which its inhabitants work it.

Among the elven peoples of Glastra, Ithiren is probably the most open to the outside world. Unlike the Eldarenenil, who reject the presence of other cultures in their lands, or the Anarenenil who tend to absorb them to the point of diluting them into their own society, the Ithirenenil maintain a more ambiguous stance. Visitors are greeted with hospitality, curiosity and respect. They are offered accommodation, conversation and often admiration for the ideas they bring with them.

However, that welcome changes when the visitor decides to stay. The Ithirenenil view with suspicion those foreigners who seek to settle in their realm. While they may integrate and learn their ways, they will always be seen as something different, as a potentially destabilising presence in a society that deeply values collective equilibrium.

## The Silver Kingdom

When the Moon Elves settled in this land after the colonisation of Glastra, they were attracted by its beauty: rolling hills, luminous forests and calm shores lapped by clear seas. For generations they lived there dedicated to perfecting their arts, convinced that self-improvement was the highest form of contribution to the welfare of the community.

Long afterwards they discovered that rich veins of silver and other precious metals lay beneath their feet.

The discovery changed Ithiren forever. Recognising that they lacked the expertise to exploit these mines without damaging the land, the Ithirenenil sought the advice of the dwarves, undisputed masters of mining. Under their guidance they began to extract the metal with care, combining mining techniques with magic to preserve the stability of the land.

Silver soon began to appear everywhere: in architecture, jewellery, ceremonial tools and magical artefacts. In time, the silversmiths of Ithiren became some of the most reputable in the world, and Ithirenenil silver creations are now objects of enormous value in all the markets of Glastra.

But wealth also brought conflict.

Different noble families began to vie for control of the mines and the territories surrounding them. However, these rivalries rarely led to open violence. Disputes were resolved through political intrigue, commercial manoeuvring, marriage alliances and even the occasional theft of deeds or mining concessions.

For decades, the kingdom was divided between two major factions.

## The Two Queens System

The rivalry ended unexpectedly when the leaders of the two factions, tired of decades of feuding, decided to marry each other.

Out of that marriage came the political system that rules Ithiren to this day: the joint reign of two monarchs, known as the **Ivory Queen** and the **Jade Queen**.

This system is based on a structure of continuous learning. At all times there is a **Mentor Queen** and an **Apprentice Queen**. When a new queen is appointed, she is joined in political marriage with the reigning queen and spends several years learning to rule under her guidance. During this time she actively participates in government, but always under the tutelage of her companion.

When the Mentor Queen decides to retire - which happens after decades of reign - the Apprentice Queen becomes the new Mentor and takes under her protection a new Apprentice, through another marriage, thus restarting the cycle.

In this way, each queen spends roughly half her reign learning and the other half teaching, ensuring remarkably stable political continuity.

The matrimonial nature of this system has generated all sorts of commentary in other lands, especially as a queen may marry politically more than once in her lifetime, and always another woman half her age. In Ithiren, however, no one expects romance in these unions. They are institutional bonds whose purpose is to ensure the stability of the kingdom.

Some queens have also had consort kings, and the laws of Ithiren carefully contemplate the role they may play within the government.

Historically, the queen who serves as Mentor defines the historical period of the kingdom. This is why chroniclers speak of **Ivory Eras** or **Jade Eras**, depending on which of the two crowns holds the main leadership role at the time.

## Ithiranas, the City of Two Crowns

The capital of the kingdom is **Ithiranas**, a coastal city considered by many to be one of the most beautiful in all of Glastra.

The city is built around a dominating hill overlooking the sea. At the top of the hill are three majestic buildings that can be seen from almost anywhere in the city: the *Royal Palace*, the *Ivory Tower* and the *Jade Manor*. These three palaces symbolise the balance between the two crowns and the shared power that defines the rule of the kingdom.

Access to the top can be made by a variety of routes. Some visitors ascend long silver staircases reinforced with magic. Others use complex systems of pulleys and lifts that constantly ascend and descend the hillside. There is also a road that winds zigzagging around the hill to reach the summit.

The slopes of this hill are occupied by the palaces of the noble families of Ithiren, those lineages powerful enough to maintain residences in the capital.

Further down the hill stretches the rest of Ithiranas. Here live merchants, artists, scholars and ordinary citizens. Still, no one would mistake the city for an ordinary human city. The houses are not clustered in regular streets, but are spread out among gardens, forests and small inlets that penetrate from the sea. The result is a chaotic city fragmented into small islands of civilisation surrounded by carefully preserved nature.

Opposite the palace hill stands the **Tower of the Sea**, the most famed arcane university of the kingdom. This is where the greatest magical power in Ithiren is concentrated, and where those mages able to afford its prestigious - and expensive - training come to study.

## The Ithirenenil and the World

The Moon Elves are deeply curious about other cultures. They enjoy hearing stories from distant lands, learning new techniques and comparing philosophical perspectives.

However, this curiosity is accompanied by an almost instinctive caution.

The philosophy that dominates Ithiren holds that ndividual improvement is indispensable but only has value when it benefits the collective Any personal ambition that jeopardises this balance is seen as dangerous.

Many Ithirenenil believe that foreigners, however well-intentioned, bring with them ideas that do not always fit this worldview. This is why initial hospitality often turns into distance when a visitor decides to stay.

This suspicion is not enshrined in any law. It is simply a deeply ingrained cultural attitude. It is not uncommon for some Ithirenenil to cross the street when they meet a foreigner, or to assume that sooner or later they will end up breaking some tradition they do not yet understand, and are often surprised when a foreigner does act according to the customs, no matter how long this "foreigner" has been living there.

Nevertheless, Ithiren remains one of the places where people from all over Glastra can most easily be found sharing a table, music or conversation under the moonlight.
