# Eldaren

- Capital: [[Eldanas\|Eldanas]]
- Population: Eldarenenil Elves (majority), Fauns, Firbolg, Gnomes, other minorities.

## The Land of the Stars

Eldaren, located immediately east of Lakua, is home to the *Eldarenenil*, known in the rest of Glastra simply as _Star Elves_ or _Elves of the Stars_. In their own language, the name of the country literally means _Land of the Stars_: _Elda_ is star and _ren_ is land, while _enil_ designates its inhabitants. For them, the name is not just a geographical designation, but a statement of identity. They believe that their people were born under the direct gaze of the firmament, and that the same ancient order that governs the heavens still resounds in the nature of the world.

The Eldarenenil are one of the elven peoples that emerged after the great divisions that marked the history of their race. Unlike other lineages, they placed a non-negotiable principle at the heart of their culture from the beginning: nature was not to be dominated or exploited, but understood and accompanied. For them, the highest form of magic was not that which transformed the world, but that which allowed them to commune with it.

Druidic magic thus became the spiritual ideal of their society. They saw it not as one discipline among many, but as the purest language by which a mortal mind could listen to the earth, the forest and the wind.

## An ancient disagreement

During the first centuries of Glastra's colonisation, the Eldarenenil had long discussions with other peoples who also sought to settle the continent. To the Star Elves, the attitude of the other races was troubling: they saw humans, dwarves and other cultures **using nature** as a resource, extracting wealth, territory and power from it.

They held a very different view. They believed that the natural bounty of the world should not be conquered or managed, but inhabited with respect, so that societies thrived within the balance of the environment rather than imposing themselves upon it.

Their words were listened to, debated... and ultimately dismissed. Many considered these positions naïve, even ridiculous. In the eyes of other cultures, the Eldarenenil vision seemed like a renunciation of progress.

Even among the elves themselves, they did not always find understanding. Their sister peoples, the Ithirenenil and the Anarenenil, saw these traditions as overly rigid, legacies of a past too ancient for the changing world that was being born on Glastra.

## The long road to isolation

Today it is undeniable that Eldaren is a deeply isolationist nation, and that its guardians can be hostile to anyone who crosses its borders without permission. However, there is no particular historical moment that marks the birth of this attitude.

There was no great war, no memorable betrayal, no broken treaty to change its destiny.

It was something slower.

Generation after generation, their warnings were ignored. Their embassies were met with condescension or indifference. As other peoples expanded their territories, Eldaren's boundaries were increasingly pushed, challenged or overstepped.

Over the centuries, Eldarenenil patience turned to caution. Caution into distrust. And distrust, finally, into a firm isolation.

They did not withdraw from the world in a single dramatic gesture. They simply stopped trying to convince it.

## A people in harmony with the forest

To many outsiders, the Star Elves have a reputation for savagery. They are said to live as creatures of the forest, far from civilisation.

The reality is quite different.

The Eldarenenil live close to nature, but that does not mean that they renounce art, refinement or architecture. Their cities do not rise up against the landscape, but grow with it.

High in the forests are great palaces made of intertwined trunks and branches that have been guided over generations into majestic structures. Among the roots of the great redwoods hide small, perfectly manicured dwellings, integrated into the earth with a quiet elegance.

Their fabrics are woven with patience and delicacy. Their tools show meticulous craftsmanship. Their weapons, though often light and organic in appearance, are as effective as those of any human or dwarf kingdom.

Although druidic magic is deeply respected, it is not the only magic present among them. Among the Eldarenenil are devout clerics of Aureon and Mireon, bards who preserve the stories of the people, sorcerers whose talents are born of blood, and scholars of other forms of magic. Their culture is neither primitive nor closed to knowledge; it simply chooses its place in the balance of the world.

## The geography of Eldaren

Eldaren is a vast expanse of forest-covered mountains and deep green valleys. The mountain ranges that criss-cross the region trap the clouds that roll in from the sea and keep them suspended between their peaks for days at a time.

Rainfall is frequent, and morning mists cover the forests like silent blankets. As a result, the vegetation grows with extraordinary strength. Trees reach colossal heights and the valleys are filled with rivers, waterfalls and mossy glades.

This constant climate of moisture and clouds makes the night sky, when it finally clears, particularly spectacular. Many believe that it is on these nights, when the stars shine over the damp mountains and moon-silvered forests, that the Eldarenenil remember why their land is so named.

## Eldanas and the Speaker of the Stars

The capital of the country is **Eldanas**, though its exact location is one of Eldaren's best kept secrets. No foreign map shows its position, and no one outside its borders has ever seen the city with their own eyes.

It is said that Eldanas lies in the heart of the mountain forests, where the trees are so old that some were already growing when Glastra emerged from the ocean.

However, the idea of a capital city in Eldaren does not work in the same way as in other kingdoms. Eldarenenil society is not organised in large cities or centralised structures. Instead, the country is made up of countless elven settlements scattered across forests, valleys and mountains. Some of these settlements are permanent, built in the treetops or among the roots of ancient forests. Others are mobile communities that slowly roam the lands of Eldaren, moving with the seasons or following the natural rhythms of the environment.

Each of these settlements has its own **Speaker**. The Speaker is not a ruler in the traditional sense, but a figure who acts as a spokesperson for the settlement, someone who listens to the community, interprets their needs and speaks on their behalf. He is also often the one who maintains the spiritual link between the group and the land it inhabits.

Above them all is a singular figure: the **Speaker of the Stars**.

Far from being a monarch or an absolute ruler, the Speaker of the Stars is rather a leader among leaders, a first among equals. His voice is heard when it is necessary to coordinate decisions that affect all of Eldaren, or when the various communities must speak with one voice to the outside world.

In quiet times, his role is discreet. Each settlement maintains its autonomy and its own traditions. But when the balance of the land is threatened, or when Eldaren must act as one people, the Speakers look to the stars... and to the one who has been chosen to speak on their behalf.

For the rest of Glastra, the figure of the Speaker of the Stars remains almost legendary. None have left Eldaren for generations, and their existence is known only from the tales of the few travellers allowed to cross its borders, or from the stories told by the rare Eldarenenil who choose to leave their homeland.

Thus, while other elven peoples follow thrones and crowns - with emperors or queens ruling over entire nations - the Star Elves remain true to a much more choral tradition: hundreds of voices speaking for their communities, and one voice that, when necessary, speaks for them all.
