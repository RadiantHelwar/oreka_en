# Cínere

**Capital:** [[Reog\|Reog]] 
**Government:** Federal river democracy with a two-tier system.
**Inhabitants:** Humans (majority), Firbolg, Lizardfolk, Dinosaurs (dominant fauna on plateaus), diverse river and canyon communities.

Cynere is a land of contrasts marked by two great canyons running north-south: the **Viridran** to the east and the **Obsidran** to the west. The areas surrounding these rivers are fertile and densely populated, while the high plateaus between them retain a more arid and wild character.

Today's region came into being after the geological collapse caused by the disappearance of the Tree of [[Regions/Glastra/Ilios\|Ilios]] after the [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]]. As the roots that supported the soft soil of Cinere were lost, the land sank, forming the two great canyons and channelling water back into them. This event transformed an ancient feudal kingdom into a new nation.

## History

Before the catastrophe, Cinere was a feudal kingdom known for dinosaur breeding and an established aristocratic tradition. However, the disaster destroyed its political structure.

The surviving royal family moved to [[Regions/Glastra/Lakua/Lakua\|Lakua]] where today they maintain a lower aristocratic status without great influence.

The survivors who remained in Cinere, in the absence of divine legitimacy or undisputed sovereign lineage, chose not to restore the monarchy. They decided to create a representative system based on a balance between communities.

Thus the river federal model was born.

## Political System

Cínere functions as a federation of cities and villages distributed along the rivers.

The central government is organised through a Federal Council, composed of representatives of the riverine communities, who elect a Chief.

The electoral system is based on **two rounds**:

1. First phase: open vote, weighted by population. 
	The two candidates with the highest number of votes are selected.
2. Second phase: final vote between these two candidates. 
	In the event of a tie, the vote of [[Reog\|Reog]] acts as a tie-breaker.

Legislatures last five years.

The system seeks a balance between regional representation and central stability.

## Geography

The two great rivers of Cinerea structure the life of the country:

- [[Viridran\|Viridran]] - eastern river, where Reog is located.
- [[Obsidran\|Obsidran]]  - river of the west, where Brega is located, a city that symbolically competes with Reog in importance.

The lowlands along the rivers are fertile and highly habitable. 
The high plateaus, on the other hand, are drier and more dangerous, but are home to exceptional resources.

The region has underground aquifers, temporary springs and tectonic pressure phenomena that generate natural geysers. These geysers carry materials from deep layers of rock, including precious metals and, above all, the coveted [[Ilic Amber\|Ilic Amber]], the crystallised residue of the ancient Ilios Tree.

The [[Ilic Amber\|Ilic Amber]] is extremely valuable and finite, since the tree is no longer there to produce it. Its extraction has generated a veritable "amber fever", attracting companies, adventurers and explorers to the highlands, although for the most part those suffering from "amber fever" are people who have decided to gamble their luck in the inhospitable highlands in order to turn their lives around with an "easy" fortune.

The highlands are inhabited by dinosaurs and other dangerous creatures, mostly poisonous and aggressive, making mining a risky venture.

In addition, architectural remnants of the ancient kingdom of Cinere survive on the plateaus, eroded by time and weather. These remains are a valuable archaeological resource, and rumours of ancient artefacts abound, attracting mercenaries and explorers.

## Culture

Cínere's identity is deeply tied to water.

A large part of the population lives from river trade, riverside agriculture and navigation.

The local people have an ambivalent relationship with the [[Ilic Amber\|Ilic Amber]]. Although foreigners seek it for wealth, there is an ancient cultural sentiment among the natives that associates it with bad luck or with the times of the fall of the kingdom.

Few Cinerans actively participate in the amber rush, though inns, shops and guide services thrive on the steady stream of prospectors.

The people of Cínere are first and foremost creators. After generations of feudal structures and inherited obligations, the reconstruction of the territory not only built cities, but also transformed mentalities. They discovered in their own work a form of dignity, and in their trades a way of personal expression. Carpenters who carve invisible filigree into structural beams, blacksmiths who compete for the most elegant hinge, weavers who turn blankets into aesthetic manifestos. In Cínere, the boundary between profession and hobby is blurred: every citizen is expected to cultivate at least one art, not for prestige, but for satisfaction. Their constructions are often eccentric, experimental, the fruit of a constant search for the most ingenious method or the most beautiful finish. For the Cinerenses, creating is not a luxury; it is tangible proof that they no longer work for a master, but for themselves.

> [!quote] Sazzalazz of the Green Scales, on Cínere
> "Cinere is the only place where a carpenter will argue with you for an hour about the perfect angle of a railing... and then take you out to dinner to celebrate. I know this first hand, I bought a house in Brega".


## Foreign Relations

Cynere maintains friendly treaties with neighbouring countries, but few are actually interested in expanding their territory into the Highlands.

The Highlands are home to a number of expeditions and companies involved in the extraction of [[Ilic Amber\|Ilic Amber]]. Many come from outside Cínere, although there are also local guides who specialise in crossing the plateaus.
