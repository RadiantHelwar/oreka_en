# Irrigos

## The Dominion that Rules the Roads

Irrigos is first and foremost a maritime Dominion.

Its banners fly from the masts of Oreka's most numerous fleets, and its ships cross routes that others consider impossible. From its ports depart convoys laden with grain, iron, spices, wine, wood, textiles, relics, promises and rumours. Nothing travels by sea without Irrigos knowing about it. Little travels without Irrigos taking part.

Its navy is not just powerful: it is constant. It patrols, it escorts, it charts, it trades. Wherever there is a coastline, there is likely to be an Irrigos dock or an agreement signed with a deep blue sealing wax seal. The great trading companies vie for size and prestige, financing ever more ambitious expeditions and competing to open new routes.

The dominion is united under a royal house that acts as a political lynchpin and symbol of stability, but real power is shared between proud regions and influential companies. Irrigos is not a conquering empire; it is an dominion of contracts.

And at sea, few dare to challenge it.


## Origins among the Mountains

Irrigos was not born facing the ocean, but enclosed between mountains.

Its territory was fragmented into isolated valleys, each abundant in one thing and lacking in almost everything else. Some possessed dense forests and excellent timber; others, veins of iron and stone; further south, vineyards thrived on impossible terraces; in certain coastal enclaves there were salt marshes and natural harbours protected by cliffs.

No region could survive on its own.

So they began to trade. First through dangerous mountain passes, then by roads and formal alliances. Caravans became customary. Trade ceased to be an urgent necessity and became a shared identity.

When they finally looked to the sea, they knew how to do it. They had learned over generations that wealth was not in owning everything, but in connecting what others needed.

The ocean was not a break with their past, but its natural extension.

The union of the regions under one crown was not the result of heroic conquest, but of a pragmatic pact: if trading together had made them prosper, ruling together would make them strong.


## Idiosyncrasy of a People on the Move

Irrigos is a dominion accustomed to the comings and goings of people.

In its ports, accents from all continents mingle. Foreign fashions are adopted without fuss. Spices arrive here earlier than anywhere else. Temples live wall to wall, and it is not uncommon for a captain to swear by a foreign god as naturally as he toasts the local harvest.

They are open, but not naive.

They value the word given, but they value the contract signed even more. They respect diversity, but expect reciprocity. Hospitality is broad; betrayal, unforgettable.

Underneath this plurality beats an intense pride. The Irrigans know they are essential to Oreka's economic balance. Their shipyards are a source of regional rivalry and national prestige. Every new ship launched is celebrated as a victory. Each successful expedition raises the name of its home port.

Regions compete with each other for numbers, routes and recognition. Who contributes the most to the royal treasury? Which city moves the most goods? Which company has reached the furthest lands? Such competition rarely breaks the unity of the kingdom, but keeps it in constant tension. Irrigos is strong, but it is never completely calm.

## The Link with Adamas

Beyond the sea, on the continent of [[Regions/Trabia/Trabia\|Trabia]] the Kingdom of [[Regions/Trabia/Adamas\|Adamas]] endures.

The royal houses of the two have been related for generations, and the bond between them is more than diplomatic. They see themselves as brotherly peoples separated by ocean. Where one prospers, the other grows stronger. Where one bleeds, the other responds.

Since the [[Lore/Wave of Darkness/Wave of Darkness\|Wave of Darkness]] that bond has become almost sacred.

Irrigos is responsible for collecting donations from all over Glastra: food, weapons, metals, resources, volunteers. Their fleets transport this flow to Adamas, sustaining theit efforts to contain the Immortal Army beyond the [[Regions/Trabia/Pagos Glacier\|Pagos Glacier]]. They do it with almost ritual efficiency, as if each crossing were part of an ancestral duty.

In Irrigos, one thing is crystal clear: if Adamas falls, the balance of the world cracks. And no port, however protected, will survive a world without balance.

That is why their ships keep sailing.

Always.
