# The Tisaran Theocracy

- **Capital**: [[Regions/Glastra/Deepreach\|Deepreach]]
- **Population**: [[Especies/Zaharak\|Zaharak]] (majority), Lizardfolk, Faunos

## The Waiting Nation

The Tisaran Theocracy is a spiritual domain settled on the northern coast of Oreka, where faith and government are inseparable. It is neither an expansionist kingdom nor a military power, but a community organised around one promise: the return one day to [[Regions/The Moons/Lehar\|Lehar]], the Green Moon.

Ruled by a religious hierarchy that guards the remnants of the so-called Tisaran Rainbow, the Theocracy is defined less by what it possesses than by what it expects. Its cities, open to the sky, rise between cliffs and dense jungles, and its architecture shines with a material unique in the world: Tisaran Glass. For its inhabitants, each shard is a memory and a sign. To the rest of Oreka, it is one of the most extraordinary arcane resources ever known.

## Origin and Belief

According to Zaharak lore, when Tisara allowed her people to abandon [[Regions/The Moons/Lehar\|Lehar]] she did so by stretching a rainbow between the moon and Oreka. The bridge would only exist for a limited time and could never be recreated. Whoever crossed it would cross it forever.

Those who descended knew that the journey had no return.

Upon arrival, they did not immediately settle at the place of descent, but dispersed throughout the world, fulfilling the mandate to live with other races and peoples. As generations passed, some began to long for a return to their lunar homeland. They believed that if they returned to the point where the rainbow had touched down and kept the devotion alive, perhaps the way could be opened again.

Out of that longing the Theocracy was born.

It is important to note that this story belongs to the Zharak faith. There is no empirical record of a bridge between worlds in any historical archive of other nations. However, the presence of the material known as [[Lore/Tisaran Glass\|Tisaran Glass]] in the region of the alleged descent is a fact that even the most sceptical cannot ignore.


## Location and Relationships

The Tisaran Theocracy is located on the northern coast of Oreka, between [[Regions/Glastra/Haizea\|Haizea]] and [[Regions/Glastra/Irrigos\|Irrigos]] and north of [[Regions/Glastra/Bardhore\|Bardhore]]. Its territory combines steep cliffs, cold seas and jungle valleys where humidity and greenery contrast with the bare rock of the coast.

It maintains friendly treaties with the three neighbouring nations and participates actively in the Glastran community, sending delegates when the situation calls for it. It does not isolate itself from the world, but neither does it dilute itself in it. Its geographical position makes it a point of transit and balance between maritime powers and inland regions.

Although some peoples view its control over the Prismatic Glass with suspicion, the Theocracy is rarely hostile. Their priority is not expansion or the accumulation of power, but the guardianship of the place where, according to their faith, heaven touched earth.

And preparation for the day when it does so again.

## The High Theocrat

The supreme leader of the Theocracy holds the title of _High Theocrat_ of Tisara.

His authority is based on the recognition of the other theocrats and his understanding of the tradition, scriptures and cycles of the Glass of Tisara. He does not rule by imposition, but by ritual consensus. His decisions are usually made after long periods of contemplation and debate in the Inner Circle, composed of the most senior theocrats.

The High Theocrat resides in [[Regions/Glastra/Deepreach\|Deepreach]], the building is partially integrated into the terrain, built around smaller fragments of glass. He does not live in isolation, but neither does he live in ostentation. His figure is public in ceremonies and pilgrimages, especially during the vigils commemorating the Descent.

Absolute serenity is expected of him. Losing his cool in public is considered a sign that the weight of the office has overtaken the individual.
