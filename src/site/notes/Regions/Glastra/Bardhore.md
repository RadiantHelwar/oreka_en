# Bardhore

**Capital:** [[Solvagrad\|Solvagrad]] 
**Inhabitants:** Dwarves (majority), Forged, Gnomes, Goliath and other mountain communities.

Bardhore is the oldest dwarven kingdom on Glastra. Founded at the beginning of the Age of the Sun by the god Lureas Nizsol, it was settled in the northern mountains of today's Lakua region, around the ancient massifs that dominate the continent. From their depths, the dwarves developed a culture based on engineering, tradition and self-sufficiency.

During its first centuries, Bardhore was a haven for those who preferred life under the rock and the patient working of stone. Collaboration between dwarves and other mountain peoples resulted in one of the most advanced societies in underground architecture in Oreka.

## Founder and the Dwarven Pantheon

Lureas Nizsol is the leader of what other species call the Dwarven Pantheon, but the people of Bardhore call Primarchs: a group of founding figures who still influence the identity of the realm today.

The god Lureas married a mortal Dwarf, and from that union the Primarchs, the gods of the Dwarven religion, were born. After the death of his first wife, Lureas retired from public life, taking his divine offspring with him and leaving rule to his mortal descendants. However, internal tensions between tradition and openness led to the departure of numerous non-Dwarven communities.

Centuries later, Lureas descended again from the Celestial Plane in a time of crisis, and after taking a new wife, he saved the kingdom of Bardhore one last time in person. Upon his return, he instigated a political restructuring that consolidated the stability of the kingdom, albeit under strict cultural rules, and strengthened the succession structure of the kingdom to ensure future stability among his mortal descendants. After this, he returned to the Celestial Plane from where he exercises his divine functions together with the rest of the Primarchs.

## Tradition and Dynasty

Bardhore deeply values family continuity and lineage. The Royal House maintains a strong connection to the direct descendants of the founder, though day-to-day governance rests with clan councils and master craftsmen.

The capital, [[Solvagrad\|Solvagrad]] is a symbol of the balance between tradition and progress: a city carved out of the living rock, half submerged in the mountain, half perched on top of it.

## Bardhore today

Bardhore maintains a stance of active neutrality in Glastra's external conflicts. While respecting trade and diplomatic alliances, it prioritises internal stability and the preservation of its independence.

Relations with neighbouring empires and kingdoms are often based on technical agreements, exchange of knowledge and trade in metals, gems and underground technology.

## The Scions of Bardhore: The Forged

The Forged were created during [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]], as constructs of Ilios wood and metal forged and engraved with arcane runes, to defend the borders of Bardhore. Dwarven ingenuity succeeded in creating an unparalleled army of automatons.

They existed as just that, mere automatons, until the Dark Wave. Menras never attacked Bardhore, but he appeared one day in one of its broadest squares and emitted an amber-hued light that spread across the land, and left without a word. The once blue eyes of the Forged flashed amber for a second, then acquired different tones depending on the individual, and they began to ask questions that made their owners uncomfortable: Who am I? Why? Am I alive?

There was no great conflict. Soon the dwarves abandoned the idea of subjugating their now sentient servants, and saw the advantages of simply adopting them as "children". They taught them how to support themselves and how to create their own offspring, while developing a second category of Forged, the "Warforged", made partly from wood not native to Ilios. These Warforged were not as useful, nor did they live nearly as long as their older brethren, but neither did they have a will of their own, nor would they oppose their masters.

Forged live mostly in Bardhore but can be found all over the world in small numbers.
