# Evisnos

- **Capital**: [[Regions/Glastra/Bronzehold\|Bronzehold]]
- **Population**: Dragonborn (majority), Kobold (majority), Humans, Halfling, Zaharak

# Evisnos

## The Confederacy of the Hills

[[Regions/Glastra/Evisnos\|Evisnos]] is a vast region of rolling hills and quiet forests where dragons and mortals live together under an ancient pact. It is neither an empire nor a centralised kingdom, but a *Draconic Commonwealth*: a union of domains ruled by different dragons who recognise a common authority, the **Governor of Evisnos**.

The majority of its population is composed of dragonborn, with kobold communities spread throughout the territory, followed by humans, halfling and zaharak. These people do not live in bondage to their draconic masters, as some foreign legends relate, but in a relationship of reciprocity: respect and care in exchange for protection.

The current Governor is the ancient bronze dragon **Ilmathar**, whose longevity has allowed him to witness countless disputes and reconciliations between his peers. His office does not imply absolute supremacy. He is mediator, arbitrator and external representative; the first among equals. This by implication to the other dragons. To the rest of the mortals of Evisnos, he is an undisputed leader.

## Origin of the Confederacy

When the lands of Evisnos were still unnamed, numerous dragons decided to establish their domains there. The hills reaching to the horizon and the wide skies offered enough space to coexist without constant warfare. Dragonborn and kobolds settled next to them, forming communities linked to each dragon, and later the other races migrated there.

Many wyrms were content to receive veneration and tribute, and were unconcerned with the battles and disagreements of others unless they involved them. It was a brass dragon, Maiagaln, who realised that they must regulate themselves or the land of dragons would eventually consume itself. So he took the decisive step: he began to organise the mortals, to mediate disputes between dragons and to provide a structure that would prevent pride from destroying the balance.

The other dragons reluctantly accepted Maiagaln's appointment as "Governor" at first, wary of appointing one of them above the others. But they quickly realised that Maiagaln was taking on administrative tasks for which none of them had the patience or motivation, and that the unified front he maintained had its advantages.

Since then, when a Governor falls or retires, dragons willing to participate in the Confederate order gather to elect a successor. Traditionally it has been brass or bronze dragoons who assume the role, more by temperament than by written law.


## The Dragons of Evisnos

Evisnos is home to dragons of all kinds: metallic, chromatic, neutral, benevolent or ambitious. The Confederation does not automatically exclude those of a darker nature, as long as they respect the internal balance.

Conflicts are inevitable. Territorial disputes, personal rivalries or expansionist ambitions can lead to real wars between wyrms. When two dragons fight, the sky becomes a battlefield, and their mortal followers are swept away by the tide.

The role of the Governor is to prevent these disputes from fragmenting the country.

Currently, the greatest tension comes from the blue dragon **Levin'Theos**, who has driven a rival blue dragon and a silver one from adjoining regions in clearly expansionist moves. Although he has not openly challenged [[Ilmathar\|Ilmathar]], its territorial consolidation is of concern to the Draconic community as much as to mortals.

Ilmathar calls for calm. He claims to have seen similar cycles fade with time. But he watches Levin'Theos with caution.

The balance of Evisnos depends on no one dragon believing it can defeat all others together.


## The Clans

Mortal society on Evisnos is organised into clans.

Each clan belongs to a particular region and owes allegiance to the dragon that rules that territory. They are relatively autonomous communities, with their own traditions, customs and specialisations. Some are distinguished by their craftsmanship, others by their martial tradition, others by their scholarship or their connection to the land.

They all share a common element: they honour their dragon and send a representative to the Council of Sages in the capital.

This council acts as an intermediary between the clans and the Governor. It manages administrative matters, conveys decisions, coordinates crisis responses and executes tasks delegated by Ilmathar. It does not rule over the dragons, but it does articulate the collective will of mortals.

### The Coming of Age Journey

Almost all clans share a fundamental tradition: upon coming of age, their young must temporarily leave home.

The form varies from clan to clan:
Some travel in groups accompanying trading caravans. 
Others leave alone to visit relatives in distant lands. 
Some must prove their worth by facing particular challenges or serving as squires to veteran knights.

The underlying purpose is the same: to get to know the other clans, to weave bonds, to understand the breadth of Evisnos. It is not uncommon for these journeys to culminate in marriage alliances between different clans, thus strengthening the cohesion of the country.

It is a deliberate social mechanism to avoid isolation and inbreeding stagnation.


## Culture and Vocation

Many dragonborn of Evisnos embrace chivalric ideals. They serve as champions of their dragon, wanderers who resolve conflicts, or guardians of roads and villages. However, not all follow the martial path.

Some clans cultivate arcane magic with discipline. Others develop druidic traditions linked to the forests and hills. Kobold craftsmanship supports much of the underground and mining infrastructure of the land.

The direct protection of dragons means that the regular army is not massive. In times of external threat, it is the wyrms themselves who tip the balance.

Each clan has its own activities and niches: those with access to vast arable land engage in agriculture or livestock, others in textiles, others in mining. There is no expectation for young people to join these particular professions, nor any social pressure, it is simply what is most accessible to each.

The clans of Evisnos value knowledge and education, and virtually all clans have compulsory schools, and the most able young people are encouraged to attend the universities in the larger cities.

The Dragonborn have large clans of sorcerers, and this arcane blood is a source of great pride for their families, and the reason for many arranged marriages, or disputes between Dragons for dominance of a clan with arcane potential. Such is the Dragonborn's appreciation of magic that even those not fortunate enough to carry it in their blood commonly study it, and not a few can cast at least low-level spells. Others devote their lives to arcane study, in the Towers of Sorcery, where wizards can learn, sorcerers can train and bards can practice. They are multidisciplinary places, despite what their name might suggest.

## Religion

The majority of the people of Evisnos follow the faith of [[Secretos/Cabal/Aureon\|Aureon]].

However, there are also devotees of draconic deities, who follow the example of the dragons they serve. These deities are:

- [[Bahamut\|Bahamut]] the Platinum Dragon, associated with good.
- [[Tiamat\|Tiamat]] the Multicolour, associated with evil. 
- [[Gilean\|Gilean]] Jewel of the Stars, representative of neutrality.

The dragons themselves acknowledge that these deities are distant entities, residing on planes beyond the stars, far, far away from Oreka. They do not claim to receive direct orders, but believe that their influence guides the great cycles of the world.

The coexistence of these beliefs rarely causes open fractures, although philosophical differences may intensify existing rivalries.

Because of the fervour and tradition of the people of Evisnos, there are two goals among them that are considered highly honourable: to enter the service of a dragon, or to become a member of the knightly order of the [[Lore/Pantheons/Seven Swords\|Seven Swords]] of Aureon.


## Foreign Relations

Evisnos maintains affable relations with the simple people of [[Regions/Glastra/The Shires\|The Shires]], Ilmathar has a special affection for his neighbouring country. The Governor even has a residence there and it is not uncommon for him to be away temporarily to escape his responsibilities and enjoy the simple pleasures of his neighbours.

It also maintains close ties with [[Regions/Glastra/Ithiren\|Ithiren]], the silver kingdom of the elves.

A small group of individuals, among them Ilmathar himself, is allowed to enter the [[Regions/Glastra/Eldaren\|Eldaren]] and is welcome within its borders, a rare privilege.

Evisnos does not seek territorial expansion. Its stability depends more on internal balance than external conquest.

And as long as the skies above the hills remain wide, dragons and mortals will continue to share them.
