# Ieró

Iero is the spiritual capital of the [[Lore/Pantheons/Church of Aureon\|Church of Aureon]] and, for many worshippers, the religious heart of all Oreka. For centuries it was considered the moral and political centre of Glastra, a place to which pilgrims, nobles and rulers flocked for spiritual guidance. Although its direct influence has waned in recent times, the city remains one of the most sacred sites on the continent.

The city is perched on the side of a mountain that once rested against the massive trunk of the Tree of [[Regions/Glastra/Ilios\|Ilios]]. Today the tree has disappeared, and in its place stretches the vast region of [[Regions/Glastra/Lakua/Lakua\|Lakua]] But Ieró remains a vivid reminder of that time. From a distance, the town looks like an ocean of golden roofs stretching down the slope, crowned at its highest point by the immense silhouette of [[Sanctum\|Sanctum]], the Great Cathedral.

Despite the political and economic changes of the last centuries, Iero remains a city proud of its heritage. Its streets breathe devotion, tradition and a constant feeling that, in this place, the divine is a little closer to the mortal world.

## The First Temple

According to Church tradition, Iero was born around the **First Temple of Aureon**.

The sacred chronicles state that this temple was erected at the exact spot where the hero Aureon bade farewell to Ilios before his Apotheosis, the moment when he ascended to the Divine Plane and became a deity. That first shrine was but a small stone building, humble and austere, but its walls were said to still contain some of the golden radiance that Aureon left behind when he left the mortal world.

Over the centuries the city grew up around this sacred place. Pilgrims, priests and craftsmen settled near the temple, and Iero gradually became a religious metropolis.

Today, the remains of the First Temple still exist, but they are no longer in the open. Its cracked walls are preserved inside the immense cathedral known as [[Sanctum\|Sanctum]], protected and venerated as a sacred relic.

The golden light that emerges from the ancient stones continues to glow softly. It passes through the cathedral's thousands of stained glass windows and is dispersed throughout the interior of the building in a constant spectacle of colours and reflections. The walls of Sanctum thus become a gigantic, ever-changing kaleidoscope of light, which many of the faithful interpret as a reminder of the eternal presence of Aureon.

There is a particular corner of Sanctum that everyone longs to reach: **The Current of Light**. In the heart of the cathedral, as close as possible to the remains of the First Temple, stands a wall of smoky crystal. It does not allow one to see through to the other side, yet it lets the iridescence of the First Temple shimmer upon it. This light seems to flow like a current of colors rising along the crystal wall, and any contact with it disturbs the current, just as introducing an object would disturb a river. Therefore, it is believed that if one places their hand on **The Current** while praying to Aureon, the light will carry their words to Him and will be answered immediately.

## Sanctum and the Seven Swords

Sanctum overlooks Iero from the top of the mountain.

Surrounding the great cathedral are the seven walled castles of the [[Lore/Pantheons/Seven Swords\|Seven Swords]], the seven holy military orders that serve as the armed wing of the Church of Aureon. Each castle is an independent fortress, capable of withstanding long sieges, and is occupied by one of the seven holy orders.

The fortresses are connected to each other by a complex network of walls, passages and movable gates that can be opened or closed at the will of their guardians. For those who do not know the exact layout of these corridors, moving between the castles can be labyrinthian.

The only safe access to Sanctum for pilgrims is through one of these castles. Traditionally, each fortress opens its gates for a few hours a day, several days a month, allowing the faithful to ascend to the cathedral. While waiting their turn, hundreds of pilgrims can stay inside the castle walls, where dormitories, chapels and courtyards have been built to accommodate visitors.

The result is that the ascent to Sanctum is almost like a small pilgrimage within the city itself.

## The Golden City

Below the sacred fortresses lies the city proper.

Four major roads lead into Iero from the outside: one from the east, one from the west, and two more from the southeast and southwest. These roads form the four main axis of the city, and from them unfolds a network of increasingly narrow and convoluted streets, like the threads of a spider's web that covers the entire mountainside.

The streets are full of religious symbols. Small chapels can be found on many corners: sometimes just a bust of Aureon accompanied by a holy water font, sometimes a small niche carved in the stone in the shape of the Wheel of the Pantheon.

Minor churches dedicated to the various aspects of Aureon also abound. Some function as centres of worship, while others are dedicated to more mundane but equally traditional activities: monasteries that brew beer, mead, sweets or preserves using produce from the vast agricultural lands the Church owns throughout Oreka.

Many visitors call Iero the Golden City. The name comes both from the abundance of golden details on sculptures and religious symbols and from the many roofs covered with gold colored tiles. During certain times of the day, when the sunlight falls on the town, the entire hillside seems to glow as if it were covered in gold.

The clothing of the clergy further reinforces this impression. The priests of Aureon often wear white robes decorated with golden embroidery, and the barley fields surrounding the city ripple in the wind like sun-coloured seas.

Despite such visible wealth, theft or vandalism is extremely rare. Not only because of the devotion of the inhabitants, but because the **Seven Swords** are always nearby, and the prisons of Iero are famed for their severity. Those who commit sacrilege rarely repeat the mistake.

## Power and Politics

Iero is ruled by the [[Lore/Pantheons/Church of Aureon\|Church of Aureon]] whose supreme leader is the **Hierophant**.

However, the Hierophant rarely deals with the day-to-day administration of the city. These tasks fall to several high-ranking cardinals. One of them acts as the civil administrator of Ieró, effectively playing the role of mayor. Another heads the **Guard of Sanctum**, an armed force dedicated to protecting the cathedral, senior members of the clergy and the most sacred places in the city, independent from the Seven Swords.

For centuries, Ieró was also an economic power. The nations of Glastra sent generous donations to the Church, both out of devotion and tradition. These tributes helped to finance temples, holy armies and public works throughout the region.

In recent times, however, the nature of these donations has changed.

Kingdoms continue to send tithes to Iero, but now they are often accompanied by expectations and conditions. Diplomatic favours, religious decisions or political support are increasingly negotiated as part of these contributions. Officially nothing has changed: they remain voluntary donations to the Church. But many in Ieró know that the balance of power is slowly shifting towards those who send the gold, rather than those who receive it.

Still, as long as Sanctum continues to shine over the city and pilgrims continue to ascend its sacred castles, Ieró will remain one of the most influential places in all of Oreka.
