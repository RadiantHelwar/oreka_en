# Serra

Serra is a fragmented, wild and dangerous continent, made up of large islands, treacherous seas and lands where the law of the strongest often prevails. These shores are home to clans, hordes and warring societies, as well as organisations that prefer to operate far from the gaze of empires and religions.

Life on Serra is marked by constant conflict. Alliances are fragile, direct violence is a daily tool, and force often decides the fate of entire villages. For many, Serra represents a land of brutal opportunity: glory, spoils or ruin await those who dare to claim something as their own on its beautiful and dangerous shores.

## The Tearing of Serra

In ancient times, Serra was not an archipelago. It was a single continent, solid and continuous, like the other territories of Oreka.

Everything changed when **Ilios emerged**.

When the great world tree rose from the depths, it dragged with it huge portions of the seabed. Those lifted lands would later become what is known today as **Glastra**. The event was catastrophic for the entire world.

Sea levels rose violently, generating tsunamis that devastated the coasts of the existing continents. Tectonic plates shifted, volcanoes awoke and earthquakes of unimaginable magnitude shook the surface of the world.

That the planet survived that cataclysm was largely the work of Ilios' own nature. As the living heart of nature, the great tree contained some of the forces unleashed by its own birth.

Serra, however, could not be protected in the same way.

The continent was literally **ripped apart**. The tectonic plate that supported it fractured and began to disintegrate, releasing immense volcanic activity. Over the centuries, those fractures formed a chain of archipelagos born of magma and fire.

## The Five Belts

The tectonic faults that split Serra fanned out from east to west, creating five great rows of islands known today as **the Five Belts**.

Each belt is actually a long chain of archipelagos formed along ancient faults in the earth's crust. Some of these islands are still volcanically active, while others have been eroded by the seas over millennia.

These belts form the geographical skeleton of Serra, separating dangerous seas, unpredictable straits and sea routes that only the most experienced navigators dare to cross.

## A Wounded Paradise

Serra's volcanic lands are extraordinarily fertile. Even after the arrival of **the Rot**, many of its islands remain abundant in natural resources, dense forests and rich soils.

Yet for those who remember what the world was like before the catastrophe, Serra is also a profoundly sad place. Much was lost when the continent was torn apart, and even today that loss continues to be felt.

What remains is a wild beauty: a fractured paradise where life continues to thrive... though always on the edge of the abyss.
