# Ergo

- Capital: [[Anaranas\|Anaranas]]
- Inhabitants: Anarenelin (Sun Elves), Humans, Dragonborn, Leonin, and other races integrated under the Imperial system.

## Origins

The Empire of Ergo was founded by the Anarenelin, the Sun Elves, after they left Serra when their former home was devastated and fragmented. Unlike other elven branches that settled in Glastra, the Anarenelin crossed over to Tomoe with the intention of building a nation that reflected their own unique philosophy: love of life, collective discipline and absolute respect for the natural balance.

During its first centuries, Ergo was an introspective and culturally cohesive society. It welcomed visitors with hospitality, but jealously guarded its identity. Full integration into Ergonian society required total commitment to its values and customs, something not everyone was willing to accept.

## Formation of the Empire

Ergo's history changed during the [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]] the nation adopted a hierarchical and functional structure, with each citizen playing a specific role within the imperial "Great Work". In the face of the crisis, the solar clergy pushed for a profound social reorganisation: the nation adopted a hierarchical and functional structure in which each citizen plays a specific role in the imperial "Great Work".

This rigid but stable caste system made it possible to coordinate the magical and agricultural effort needed to restore the ecosystem. Since then, Ergo has become one of the most advanced technological and arcane powers in Oreka.

### Government

Since the great reorganisation after the Rot, Ergo has been ruled by the [[Imperial Caste\|Imperial Caste]] a lineage of the ancient high clergy, direct descendants of the first chosen of the *Child of the Sun*, **Mill**. The figure of the Emperor or Empress not only directs the political destiny of the nation, but also holds the highest religious authority as head of the clergy.

In Ergo there is no separation between faith and government: civil order is understood as a manifestation of divine balance. Imperial leadership is not seen as a hereditary right, but as a sacred responsibility to guard abundance, coordinate the Great Work and preserve life in all its forms.

The Imperial House is supported by a council of high priests, strategists and wizards, but its final word is unquestioned, for it is vested with divine authority.

### Castes

In Ergo, no one goes hungry, every child is educated, and every citizen has a purpose.

Social mobility between castes exists, though it is exceptional and requires extraordinary merit or integration into higher-status lineages. Collective stability is valued above individual gain, and order is considered the highest form of freedom.

Mobility within one's caste is encouraged, and individuals are free to seek a vocation that suits their abilities and needs within their own caste. The system would only oppose such a choice if it created an imbalance in the Great Work—for example, if too many people left a sector to the point that it could no longer function, or if another became overcrowded.

In any case, the system itself would examine the cause of the imbalance and attempt to correct it. It would not simply impose unwanted occupations without first engaging in self-examination and attempting to resolve the underlying issue.

## Today

Over the centuries, the constant pressure to maintain abundance in Tomoe led Ergo to look beyond its borders. From their perspective, many nations waste resources, allow internal inequalities and sink into avoidable conflicts.

The Empire argues that its historical responsibility is to protect life and preserve balance, even when that means intervening in regions unable to sustain themselves. In lands where abundance is wasted or life is endangered by disorganisation, Ergo considers it legitimate to offer guidance and stability.

Two centuries ago, an intermittent conflict with [[Regions/Glastra/Kostakt/Kostakt\|Kostakt]] began, until twenty years ago, during the last war, Ergo took control of the region of the [[Regions/Glastra/Kostakt/Dartza\|Dartza]]. Following the signing of the [[Last Truce\|Last Truce]], the territory remains under imperial administration, while both nations maintain a vigilant peace.

For some, Ergo is an expansionist empire.
For others, it is proof that order and discipline can save a people from starvation and chaos.

# Caste System

## Imperial Caste

The Imperial Caste is the least numerous, but the most important caste in Ergo. It constitutes the apex of the Great Work. Its members are acknowledged descendants of the First Emperor, considered the last direct heir of the one who was appointed leader by the _Child of the Sun_.

All members of the Imperial Caste are descendants of the First Emperor, or have joined the Imperial Caste via marriage. The legitimacy of each member is assessed according to the purity and continuity of their family tree, measuring the closeness of their bloodline to the First Emperor and the number of marital or succession "detours" that separate them from him.

The Imperial House does not use surnames. Its members are identified exclusively by their title and ceremonial name, reflecting the fact that they do not represent a family in the ordinary sense, but the living embodiment of the solar order.

> [!Quote] Note
> The Imperial Caste uses a set of names traditionally reserved for the solar lineage. On rare occasions, when an Imperial newborn is given a name common to other castes, those who bear it usually modify it voluntarily. There is no law requiring this, but sharing a name with a member of the Imperial House is considered socially inappropriate. 
> The birth of the First Imperial Prince Innui 154 years ago triggered an unprecedented wave of registry changes across the Empire.

The Emperor or Empress simultaneously serves as the highest political authority in the Empire and supreme head of religion and the Imperial and Clerical Castes. 

The line of succession is not only based on the principle of primogeniture, but also on the genealogical calculation established by the [[Sosen\|Sosen]], a body composed of priests and archivists who certify dynastic legitimacy. Thus, even if an Emperor has direct descendants, it is possible that another relative may have the necessary characteristics to be considered closer to the First Emperor, and therefore appear earlier in the line of succession.

Members of the Imperial Caste possess ceremonial privileges, moral authority and diplomatic responsibilities, but are also subject to a rigorous code of conduct. Every public action of an Imperial member is considered a reflection of the Empire itself.

The stability of the main line is a top priority. Marriages are negotiated with precision, alliances are calculated with extreme care, and the preservation of the bloodline is seen as a sacred duty.

## Clerical Caste

The Clerical Caste constitutes the spiritual foundation of the Empire and the second cornerstone of the Great Work. Its members mediate between the other Castes and the Imperial Caste; they are custodians of the balance that sustains the bounty of Ergo and watch over the spiritual welfare of all members of the Empire.

From their ranks emerged the Imperial House itself during the reorganisation following [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]]. Although today the Emperor or Empress holds supreme religious authority, the Clerical Caste retains responsibility for preserving doctrine, directing rites and overseeing the proper harmony between the castes.

Members of the Clerical Caste are trained from a young age in discipline, theology and ecological magic. Many of them participate directly in the complex rituals that regenerate the land and keep Tomoe's fields fertile.

Their authority is not coercive, but moral and structural. They are counsellors, ritual judges and guardians of spiritual order.

They are the most flexible of the castes. Membership requires application and renunciation of any previous membership. Most people enter the Clerical Caste on the lowest rung of it, but others, especially if their power and influence prior to joining was considerable, may enter directly into higher positions. Mobility within the Clerical Caste is a mixture of nepotism, politics, and individual success, which does not exist in the other Castes.

## Noble Caste

The Noble Caste is composed of the great houses that administer regions, cities, and strategic resources on behalf of the Empire.

Unlike the Imperial Caste, whose legitimacy derives from direct solar lineage, the Nobility base their position on length of service and demonstrated loyalty to the Imperial House. Many of these families gained their rank after the reorganisation brought about by [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]], by taking on responsibilities critical to the survival of the Empire.

The nobility acts as an intermediary between the imperial will and the lower castes. It administers, oversees and ensures that the Great Work runs smoothly.

Their marriages are also carefully negotiated, though their purity of lineage is not measured with the same mathematical rigour as that of the Imperial House.

## Merchant Caste

The Merchant Caste includes merchants, skilled craftsmen, engineers, economic administrators and those responsible for internal and external trade.

In Ergo, trade is not understood as individual accumulation, but as efficient redistribution of resources. The Merchant Caste ensures that the abundance generated by collective effort flows correctly between regions and castes.

Its members may achieve great material prosperity, but their status is always subordinate to the overall balance of the Empire.

They are responsible for maintaining trade relations with other nations, sometimes acting as informal diplomats.

## Military Caste

This is the second most mobile house within the Ergonian structure. Here lies the combat power of the Empire. Although theory dictates that any soldier could be promoted to general or army leader, in practice, members of the Clerical or Noble Castes take these roles regularly, and all of them are subject to the Emperor's orders.

Anyone can join the Military Caste, but while there are generally no problems, recruiters may refuse an offer if they believe that the person is better off in their current job, would be a disadvantage to the army, or holds an important position that cannot be easily replaced in their community.

## Common caste

The Common Caste forms the basis of the Great Work and is the most numerous of all. It includes farmers, labourers, artists, minor officials and workers of all kinds.

Far from being considered inferior in dignity, the Common Caste is presented as the productive heart of the Empire. Each citizen receives a basic education and is assigned to a function commensurate with his abilities and the needs of the whole.

Mobility to higher castes is exceptional, but possible through extraordinary merit or family integration.

The Empire sees the Common Caste as proof that order protects even the lowliest.

## Casteless and Stripped

The **Casteless** are those who are not part of the Imperial system. Visitors, diplomats, or foreigners may temporarily reside under Ergo's protection as long as they respect its laws and customs, and they are received as honored guests. However, extended stays often require formal integration into a Caste—usually the Common Caste—or a return abroad.

The **Stripped** represent the highest possible punishment within the Empire. Instead of execution—considered an affront to the Empire’s absolute respect for life—the state applies the total removal of social recognition to those deemed irreparably harmful to the Great Work. The condemned person's name is removed from official records; their past ceases to exist in public documents, and their membership in any Caste is annulled.

Those who knew them must act as though they had never existed.

A **Stripped** individual is expelled from the lands of Ergo and is no longer considered part of its history. They live on, but with no past, no rank, and no place in collective memory—and they may never return.

## Eladrin Enclave

Near the [[Drya Lighthouse\|Drya Lighthouse]] lives a small community of Eladrin, who enjoy an environment in the vicinity of the lighthouse that more closely resembles their Feywild homeland. This enclave constitutes one of the few exceptions within the borders of Ergo where the Great Work does not apply.

The Eladrin live according to their own customs and govern themselves as they see fit. They are required only to pay a tribute similar to that demanded from the rest of the Empire’s population. As long as they remain within their enclave, they are free to organize their society and conduct their affairs as they wish.

Any Eladrin who leave the enclave, however, must submit to the laws of the Empire like all other inhabitants of Ergo.
