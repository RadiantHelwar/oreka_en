# Aasimar

The Aasimar are individuals touched by the influence of the **Benign Planes** or the **Divine Plane**. Unlike other species, they do not constitute an independent people or culture of their own. An Aasimar can be born into any society and among virtually any mortal species.

Although they share certain physical and spiritual characteristics, their general appearance usually reflects that of the species from which they originate.

## Common traits

Aasimar often possess traits that are considered beautiful or harmonious by the standards of their species. Their presence tends to be comforting to those around them, and many individuals describe a hard-to-explain sense of calm, confidence or respect when in their vicinity.

Although their lifespan does not usually differ from that of other members of their species, Aasimar maintain a youthful and vigorous appearance for most of their lives, ageing only in their later years.

However, the most recognisable feature of an Aasimar is the **halo** that floats above their heads.

## The Halo

Every Aasimar has a visible halo suspended above their head. This halo can take a variety of forms: rings of light, fragmented crowns, swirling filaments, geometric structures or patterns impossible to describe precisely. No two halos are exactly alike.

Despite its luminous appearance, the halo neither emits real light nor illuminates its surroundings. It also has no mass and cannot be touched.

This is because the halo is not a physical object, but a spiritual manifestation of the Aasimar's divine heritage. Only souls can perceive it, so its presence does not affect visibility or reveal the individual's position in the darkness.

The halo responds partially to the will of its wearer. With some effort, an Aasimar can temporarily conceal it, though this requires concentration and can rarely be maintained for long periods. When not actively concealed, the halo tends to naturally reposition itself to remain visible, always appearing above or even through any garment or headgear the Aasimar wears.

## Origin of the Aasimar

There are many circumstances that can lead to the birth or appearance of an Aasimar.

The most common is descent from other Aasimar or celestial entities, although this heritage may remain hidden for generations. Occasionally, two lineages in which the celestial influence has been diluted over time may meet again, causing it to resurface strongly in a new individual.

Other Aasimar are born due to extraordinary circumstances, such as:

- Being conceived or born on a Benign Plane.
- Receiving a divine blessing before or immediately after birth.
- Being exposed to great concentrations of positive energy.

On rare occasions, an individual may even become an Aasimar later in life. This can occur through covenants, exceptional blessings or direct absorption of celestial power.

Because of this diversity of origins, Aasimar can be found among virtually any mortal species.

## The Chrysos Clan

All members of the [[Clan Chrysos\|Clan Chrysos]] are Aasimar, and all share a unique halo manifestation.

In their case, the halo takes the form of numerous constantly rotating strands of golden light, forming a continuous flow that describes a ring above their heads. These strands intertwine and separate endlessly, as if in a perpetual motion of golden energy.

This shared trait is one of the most distinctive marks of the Chrysos lineage.
