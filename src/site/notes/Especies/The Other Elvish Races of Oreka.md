# The Other Elvish Races of Oreka

In addition to the three great Elvish peoples: the **Anarenelin** (Sun Elves), the **Ithirenelin** (Moon Elves) and the **Eldarenelin** (Star Elves); there are three other lineages born in different domains of the ancient Tree of [[Regions/Glastra/Ilios\|Ilios]].

These peoples were shaped by the extreme environments they inhabited: the heights of the clouds, the depths of the ocean and the caverns beneath the roots of the great tree. Over the millennia, and especially after the coming of [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]], their destinies took very different turns.

Some are on the verge of extinction. Others live in isolation from the rest of the world. And one of them fell into such deep corruption that today they are barely recognisable as part of the original elven people.

# Fanarenelin - The Elves of the Clouds

The **Fanarenelin** once inhabited **Fanaren**, the realm of the clouds, a domain built high in the branches of the original [[Regions/Glastra/Ilios\|Ilios]]. Their civilisation lived suspended between the air currents and the vast seas of clouds, on natural platforms formed by colossal branches and gardens fed by dew and light.

They are the only elven lineage endowed with natural wings. Their bones are hollow and their bodies light, adapted to the oxygen scarcity of extreme altitudes. For millennia they needed little sustenance: a few morsels of fruit and the condensed water of dew were enough to sustain them throughout the day, and their white wings absorbed the magical power of the branches of Ilios where they lived, providing for their needs.

The Fanarenelin not only flew by harnessing air currents. According to their own traditions, they also followed Oreka's lines of magic, invisible currents that travelled the world and that their wings were able to sense and channel.

The arrival of the Rot was devastating for their people. Their bodies absorbed much of that corruption before they were even aware of its existence, and when the Tree of Ilios disappeared, the ecosystem on which they depended collapsed completely.

Today, small isolated communities survive, tucked away on the highest peaks of [[Regions/Glastra/Bardhore\|Bardhore]] and in the mountains of the [[Regions/Glastra/Tisaran Theocracy\|Tisaran Theocracy]] where the Dwarves and Zaharak granted them refuge.

However, their new life away from ancient Ilios is slowly dooming them. Their wings no longer absorb ambient magic as before. Over time the wings of each Fanarenelin grow darker, losing the luminous white that once seemed to shine brightly and their capability to feeding them by absorving energy. Fanarenelin must eat more and more to survive, but their bodies were never designed to process large quantities of food.

Many end up dying of starvation, just as their wings turn completely black and can no longer absorb any magic to sustain them.

Today, no Cloud Elf possesses the pure wings of old, like the clouds that gave them their name. At most, they retain a dull, off-white, distant memory of their former selves, darkening over the course of their lives until they are entirely jet black, unable to absorb any sustenance.

Among their people, they fear the coming of the "black wings", which foretell death by starvation, and use their meagre resources to continue to search for a source of nourishment so that their children will not starve, and a way to purify the wings of their people.


# Earenelin - The Water Elves

The Earenelin are an elven lineage adapted entirely to life in water. They inhabit oceans, seas, rivers and great lakes throughout Oreka, from coastal reefs to depths where light barely reaches.

No one knows for sure when this adaptation arose. The oldest Elvish records already mention their existence, but none describe when the Elves left the land to embrace the water.

Earenelin have skins of blue, turquoise or sea-green, and webbed hands and feet that allow them to move with great speed and grace underwater. Their lungs can breathe air, but only for a few hours; out of the water, their bodies quickly begin to weaken.

For this reason they have very little contact with surface civilisations. Their alliances and relationships tend to be with other marine species, such as merfolk and tritons, with whom they share oceanic routes, underwater sanctuaries and fishing grounds.

Their cities, where they exist, are built among coral reefs, giant kelp forests or submerged ruins of ancient civilisations. They often prefer dispersed settlements, integrated into the marine environment rather than imposing rigid structures.

For many surface peoples, the Earenelin remain little more than a marine legend.

# Olvarenelin - The Dark Elves

The Olvarenelin lived in Olvaren, the subterranean realm beneath the roots of the Tree of Ilios. Among all the elven domains, theirs was considered the most beautiful.

The gigantic roots of the tree carried light from the surface into the deep caverns, where it filtered down in thousands of iridescent shades. The ceilings of those caverns, covered in natural crystals, reflected the light, creating an underground firmament that many described as more beautiful than the night sky.

As [[Lore/Cosmology/The Rot\|The Rot]] began its ascent from the depths of Oreka, it was Olvaren who received the first blow.

There, at the very roots of the world, the Olvarenelin struggled for decades to contain the corruption, not knowing that their fight was in vain. Finally they were defeated.

The Rot did not just devastate their cities: it settled into their very essence.

The descendants of those people are known today as **Drow**. They retain the intelligence, longevity and wit of the elves, but their nature has changed profoundly. Their underground realms are still beautiful in a dark and disturbing sense, flooded and full of impossible architecture, giant mushroom gardens and cities carved from living stone. But they are also cruel, dangerous and inhospitable places.

The Drow have little contact with the surface. Their most common relations are with the svirfneblin and duergar, who suffered similar fates to the Deep Elves, as well as the slaves they capture or trade in the deep.

However, the history of the Olvarenelin did not end entirely in corruption.

A small group managed to flee Olvaren before the Rot fully took hold. These exiled Deep Elves retained the purity of their lineage and dispersed to caves, forgotten tunnels and underground settlements throughout Oreka.

Their eyes are unaccustomed to sunlight, and they rarely leave the depths. But an ancient hope lingers among them: that one day they will be able to free their Drow brethren, or at least reclaim the realm that was once considered the most beautiful place in the world.
