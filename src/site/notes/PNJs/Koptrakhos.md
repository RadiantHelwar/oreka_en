# Koptrakhos

## _The Eternal Emperor_

It is said that Koptrakhos was born in the Age of the Sun, in a time so ancient that even the dates have lost their meaning. He was a common faun, one among many, in a land whose name no longer exists on maps. He was neither a warrior nor a scholar, nor did he possess any extraordinary talents, save his devotion to [[Lore/Pantheons/Glorinth\|Glorinth]], a minor goddess of everyday magic: that of amulets, small rituals, the evil eye and superstitions whispered in kitchens and fields.

Koptrakhos helped his people with these humble arts. He blessed crops, protected homes, undid minor ailments. And he did so with a faith so sincere that, according to the oldest traditions, the very [[Lore/Pantheons/Glorinth\|Glorinth]] came down one day to thank him for his service.

After that visit, something changed. His little arts began to work with unsettling effectiveness. Where once there was comfort, now there was certainty. Where once there was superstition, now there was result.

And that aroused fear.

He was accused of dangerous practices. Some say that the wizard who denounced him was a romantic rival; others that he detested magic beyond his control; still others that it was all a political manoeuvre. What is certain is that Koptrakhos was captured and tried by a council of wizards: he was condemned to public death.

As the executioner's axe descended, Glorinth manifested again. She could not save him directly. But she could offer him something else: In an act that would come at great cost to her, she could teach him the Ultimate Curse. In return, Koptrakhos would belong to her after his natural death. He would not take a wife. He would not seek another love. When his life ended, his soul would ascend to the Celestial Realm where she would await him.

Koptrakhos accepted.

What happened next is not clearly recorded. The chronicles speak of screams, of death, of chaos. When the dust settled, Koptrakhos was still standing.

From then on, his rise was unstoppable.

First he conquered the land that had condemned him. Then he imposed a new doctrine: no magician would ever again decide the life or death of those who had not mastered the Art. Arcane scholars would be servants of the people, not their masters. But resistance was fierce. Neighbouring kingdoms rejected this reorganisation of power. Wars ensued. And twenty years after his first coronation, Koptrakhos proclaimed himself Emperor of Demenor, an empire forged by both conquest and retaliation.

He knew he was not invulnerable. He knew that there could be those who would threaten his rule. So he placed a curse on his territory: anyone gifted with magic would suffer misfortune and maladroitness in their Craft, unless they lived and trained in the Imperial Circles and swore magical allegiance to the Emperor.

Circles rose up all over Demenor.

And as academic magic was harnessed and policed, the old traditions - the charms, the homemade blessings, the superstitions - began to disappear. That which had been at the core of Koptrakhos's devotion was cornered and persecuted, for the Emperor was afraid that another like him would emerge from those traditions, another who could stand up to him. The cult of Glorinth was weakened.

The goddess warned.

Koptrakhos did not listen.

Or maybe he listened and chose to ignore her.

The Emperor then made the one mistake he had not foreseen: he fell in love.

On his wedding day, before the entire court, as he pronounced his vows, his form changed. Glorinth cursed him at the moment when, by taking another as his wife, he broke his previous promise to her. His body became hideous. His heart became unfit to love and to be loved. And she swore that when he finally died and his soul reached the heavenly realm, the torment that awaited him would be eternal.

Koptrakhos chose not to die.

By means of forbidden arts he became an undead being. From then on he prolonged his existence for almost ten thousand years. He ruled as Eternal Emperor, feared, unchallenged, unable to inspire true affection. Glorinth's curse manifested itself in every broken relationship, in every glance that failed to find warmth.

During the [[Lore/Wave of Darkness/Wave of Darkness\|Wave of Darkness]] when the whole world was shaking, the unthinkable happened. A hungry girl slipped into the palace through the kitchens. Wandering through corridors she did not understand, she found herself in front of the Emperor.

Frightened, she threw the sack of seeds she was carrying at him.

No one knows why, the Emperor himself had erased the knowledge that explained what would happen after this act, the very knowledge that could perhaps have saved him: what followed was a month of screams.

The Emperor suffered for more than thirty days. During that time, he suffered in his own flesh the death of all those affected by his Ultimate Curse and all those times his undead nature had allowed him to overcome the end. No one could come close. No one could stop him. When the pain ceased, his body shuddered one last time and he lay still. Then a breeze blew him apart, and his remains scattered like dust.

It is said that the suffering of that month was terrible.

It is also said that it pales before what he now suffers in the realm of Glorinth.

And so ended the reign of the Eternal Emperor.
