# Dream Plane

The Dream Plane is one of the Planes of Existence and among the closest to the [[Lore/Cosmology/Mundane Plane\|Mundane Plane]].

Unlike other planes, its substance is not composed of matter or energy in the conventional sense, but of the very dreams of conscious creatures from the Mundane Plane. Every sleeping thought, memory, fear, or desire that arises during mortal rest leaves an imprint on this plane—or perhaps it is the other way around, and each fragment of this plane leaves an imprint on the dreams of mortals.

As a result, the Dream Plane is extraordinarily malleable. Distances are subjective, time can be stretched or compressed, and the environment can transform completely from one instant to the next. A castle may become a forest, an ocean an endless corridor, and a conversation may last years or mere moments.

The native inhabitants of this plane are known as **Figments**. These entities assume the roles that appear in the dreams of mortals: enemies, allies, guides, monsters, or mere background figures. Scholars debate the true nature of this relationship: it is unclear whether Figments take shape in response to mortal dreams, or whether mortal dreams merely glimpse the lives and actions of the Figments.

Figments possess multiple possible forms. During the early years of their existence, they switch between them with ease, but over time most adopt a single predominant form.

The most common forms are:

- **Nightmare Lords**
> Embodiments of mortal fears and traumas. Their function is to confront dreamers with their past, testing them during rest. Though feared, many sages believe they serve a necessary purpose: to temper the spirit of mortals.

- **Identity Guardians**
> Figments that protect dreamers from the mental deterioration that constant exposure to dream chaos could cause. Their interventions are often comforting, familiar, or protective, helping preserve the emotional stability and identity of the sleeper.

- **Night Terrors**
> Entities that feed on mortal suffering. They induce intense and disturbing nightmares, drawing energy from the fear, anguish, and despair they provoke.

- **Chaos Witchlings**
> The most common Figments. These entities exist independently of mortals, devoted to their own whims and interests. Their mere presence provokes strange, chaotic, or seemingly absurd dreams, which may produce any effect on those who experience them.

The regions of the Dream Plane closest to the Mundane Plane tend to be more coherent and stable, reflecting dreams that are structured or influenced by mortal reality. However, deeper within the plane, logic dissolves entirely, and dreams become an endless whirlwind of imagination, memory, and desire.
