# The Rot

For nearly a century, Oreka endured a period of desolation known as the **Rot**. The skies turned pale and opaque, a phenomenon known as the [[Lore/Cosmology/White Sky\|White Sky]], and the fertility of the land declined. Many creatures became deformed, and food shortages pushed entire kingdoms to the brink of war. It was an age of hunger, fear, and despair.

According to historians of the **Church of Aureon**, the Rot was caused by the [[Lore/Ancient Past/Four Fiend Kings\|Four Fiend Kings]] who, before being imprisoned by Aureon, managed to corrupt the heart of Ilios. Their influence took millennia to manifest, yet eventually it sickened the Tree, bringing disease, catastrophes, and changes to the fauna, leaving scars that persisted for generations.

## The Restoration

Divine intervention by Aureon resolved the crisis. His power, channelled through the faith of his followers, purified Oreka and restored the fertility of the land. Aureon removed the corrupted Tree and replaced it with a luminous tree of golden light, as incorruptible as it was majestic. With its appearance above the skies of Glastra, the faithful realised that their prayers had been answered and that Oreka had been granted salvation.

Thanks to this act, the Rot was almost entirely reversed. The affected regions began to recover, the skies returned to their natural hue, and the proliferation of deformed creatures ceased.

## Aftermath and Teachings

Although the Rot ended, many lands still bear scars from that period. The Church teaches that sustained and devout faith was the key to overcoming the crisis, and that as long as devotion to Aureon endures, Oreka will remain protected. The story of the Rot became a moral lesson: the salvation of the world depends on the faith and unity of its peoples.

Scholars, for their part, continue to document and study these events, but for the general population, the explanation is simple and clear: faith saved Oreka, and Aureon’s divine protection is eternal.

Today, the Lakua region in the centre of Glastra and the vast Kentron Lake occupy the site where the original, physical Tree of Ilios once stood. The entire valley marks the footprint left by Ilios when it disappeared.
