# Glorinth

## Goddess of Superciliousness, Mistress of the Little Rites

Glorinth is a minor deity associated with everyday magic: the kind not studied in academies or recorded in grimoires, but whispered among neighbours and passed down in kitchens, fields and marketplaces. She is the patron saint of protective amulets, of the evil eye, of harvest blessings and of small ritual gestures that seek to tilt fortune just one degree in favour of the practitioner. Her cult was never grand or institutional; it flourished in domestic customs and local traditions rather than in marble temples.

For millennia it was especially revered in Demenor, where its practices permeated daily life before the rise of [[NPC/Koptrakhos\|Koptrakhos]]. However, the imperial centralisation of magic and the persecution of popular traditions profoundly weakened its presence. In time, Glorinth ceased to be an overt figure of devotion and became a whispered name, almost superstitious in itself.

It is said that her heavenly realm is not a shimmering paradise, but a vast web of symbols, red threads, dangling talismans and unfulfilled promises. There the consequences of broken oaths await, and there - according to legends - he who was once their most devoted servant suffers eternally.
