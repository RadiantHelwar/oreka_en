# Lýpi

! [206](/img/user/Extras/Imagenes/PNJs/Dioses/Lypy.png)

**Titles**: Las Plañideras, Las Tres en Circulo, (La Llorona, la Llama, la Sonrisa)
**Symbol**: Inverted Teardrop
**Associated Number**: VI
**Domains**: Sadness, Sacrifice, Mourning, Spiritual Healing
**Description**: Three middle-aged ladies, commonly holding hands although sometimes depicted separately. All three have dark hair and wear simple purple robes, with a chain belt with their symbol hanging in the centre. **Deide**, La Llorona, is always depicted sad and weeping. **Geide**, the Flame, is depicted haughty, strong, and looking down on others. **Leide**, the Smile, is shown conciliatory and with a forced smile, with arms outstretched to the other two or to the petitioners.
