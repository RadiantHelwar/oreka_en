# The Seven Swords of Aureon

The **Seven Swords of Aureon** are the armed and disciplinary arm of the Church. From their strongholds around **Sanctum**, high above **Iero**, these orders protect the holy places, defend the faith and execute the decisions of the Hierophant when words and diplomacy are no longer enough.

Although from the outside they are often perceived as seven separate orders, in reality the Swords form a single institution. All answer to the spiritual authority of the Church and share the same doctrine, but each is dedicated to specific areas of service to Aureon.

Over the centuries, each Sword has developed its own culture, internal traditions and a particular reputation among the faithful.

## Structure of the Swords

Each of the Seven Swords is governed by a **Sword Prelate**, a figure that combines military, religious and administrative authority. The Prelates reside in the seven castles surrounding Sanctum and participate in the great councils of the Church when the Hierophant summons their orders.

Under them are the **Custodes**, officers charged with overseeing the various spheres of action of the orders. Unlike a conventional military hierarchy, the Custodes do not belong exclusively to a single Sword. Each Custodian serves **two different Prelates**, sharing responsibilities between two orders.

This interlocking system forms a web of intersecting authority that forces the Swords to cooperate constantly. No order has absolute control over its Dominions, and any attempt at internal conspiracy would quickly be limited by the mutual dependence between their leaders.

Church theologians often describe this system with a simple metaphor: one sword can be broken, but seven interlocking swords are almost impossible to break.


## The Dominions of Aureon

Each Sword holds two **Sacred Domains**, realms of action that reflect different facets of the faith in Aureon. These domains do not belong exclusively to a single order; each is shared by two Swords, reinforcing the collaboration between them.

The seven domains recognised by the Church are:

- Protection
- Expansion
- Erudition
- Introspection
- Punishment
- Redemption
- Salvation

It is through these domains that both the spiritual and military action of the Church is articulated.

## The Gifts of the Swords

The most devout and experienced members of each order may receive **sacred blessings** associated with the domains their Sword guards. These manifestations of power are considered gifts bestowed by Aureon through discipline and faith.

At lower ranks, these gifts often manifest as lesser talents: extraordinary stamina, heightened senses, the ability to perceive lies, or to channel divine energy.

However, the most prominent members - and especially the Custodians and Prelates - can develop truly extraordinary abilities, direct reflections of the spiritual principles they represent.

Thus, a knight of the Sword of Redemption may be able to heal impossible wounds, while an inquisitor of the Sword of Punishment may be able to invoke holy fire on the guilty.

# The Seven Swords

## The nature of the Swords

Despite the diversity of their functions - scholarship, mission, judgement, redemption or salvation - all the orders share a fundamental truth: they are swords before anything else.

Members of these orders may be scholars, diplomats, theologians, inquisitors or healers, but they are all rigorously trained in combat. Each is first and foremost a holy warrior in the service of Aureon. Their disciplines may vary - from mastery of sword and spear to divine magic or the spiritual arts of faith - but none of them are strangers to the battlefield.

The Church has never forgotten that the world contains threats that cannot be resolved by words or prayers alone. Demons from the lower planes, dark cults, creatures that defy the divine order, or even mortal armies have tried on numerous occasions to break the will of Aureon and his faithful.

That is why the orders are not named after books, pens or reliquaries.

They are **Swords**.

And those who wield them are trained to fight, whether with steel, with magic or with the very strength of their faith.


## First Sword: The Sword of the Bastion

**Dominions**: Protection and Expansion

The Sword of the Bulwark represents the active defence of the faith. Its members escort pilgrims, protect temples, and act as the first line of defence when the lands of the Church are threatened.

They are visible and respected knights, accustomed to marching on roads and frontiers. They also accompany missionaries in regions where the faith of Aureon has not yet taken root.

Among the Swords, the Presidium is closest to the classical ideal of holy chivalry.


## Second Sword: The Dyed Sword

**Domains**: Expansion and Erudition

The Inked Sword is dedicated to the applied study of knowledge. Its members run universities, fund research, and oversee missionaries who bring the faith to other regions of the world.

Unlike more strictly military orders, their ranks are filled with scholars, explorers, diplomats and even scientists. For them, understanding the world is a way of honouring God's creation.

Their name comes from the ink with which they write treatises, maps and sacred texts.

## Third Sword: The Enclaved Sword

**Domains**: Erudition and Introspection

This order guards monasteries, libraries and cloisters of spiritual study. Members of the Cloistered Sword believe that outer knowledge is only of value when accompanied by deep inner understanding.

Many of the Church's great theologians belong to this order, as well as spiritual counsellors and teachers of religious meditation.

Although they rarely engage in armed conflict, their intellectual influence within the Church is enormous.


## Fourth Sword: The Silent Sword

**Domains**: Introspection and Punishment

The Silent Sword is the most feared of Aureon's orders.

Its members investigate heresies, forbidden cults and doctrinal deviations within the Church itself. They act as judges and inquisitors when the purity of the faith is threatened.

The order's name reflects its philosophy: listen before speaking, observe before judging, and act only when the fault is clear.

Many of the faithful believe that no one knows the human soul as deeply as the knights of this sword.

## Fifth Sword: The Penitent Sword

**Domains**: Punishment and Redemption

While the Silent Sword judges, the Penitent Sword administers atonement.

Its members oversee sacred prisons, penitential rituals and processes of spiritual reconciliation. They firmly believe that even the most fallen sinner can find redemption, provided he agrees to face the consequences of his actions.

Many individuals condemned by the Church end up serving in this order as a way of atoning for their faults.

## Sixth Sword: The Radiant Sword

**Domains**: Redemption and Salvation

The Radiant Sword focuses its attention on the fight against the supernatural, especially invaders from the lower planes, or those who rub shoulders with them.

Before its hallowed blades fall all manner of enemies who threaten the sanctity of the mortal soul, and among its ranks are many who were once enemies and were saved from their dark path by the light of the Radiant Sword.


## Seventh Sword: The Merciful Sword

**Dominions**: Salvation and Protection

The Radiant Sword is the closest guard to the heart of the Church. This order represents the compassionate face of the Church.

Its members run hospitals, pilgrim shelters and relief missions in regions devastated by war or disaster. For them, saving a life is a direct way of honouring Aureon.

Among the common people, they are probably the most beloved of all the Swords.
