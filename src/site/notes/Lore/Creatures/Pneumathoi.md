# Pneumathoi


>[!quote] Excerpt from the Treatise on Wandering Souls, by the arcanist Varen Lys, 11th century GC
"The Pneuma is neither slave nor mistress; it is mirror and echo of the soul it accompanies."

Pneumathoi, or Pneumas in common speech, are outer souls of a winged nature that exist solely within the Aitherion family.

The Pneuma is born simultaneously with the Aitherion, and the two are said to be one and the same being. The soul of the human exists outside the body, manifesting as a Pneumathoi.

Most Pneumathoi take the form of birds — eagles, hawks, owls, herons— although some have appeared as insects, mammals, reptiles, or even normally wingless creatures, granted impossible flight by the Pneuma. Their appearance often reflects the true personality, fears, and virtues of their human companion.

These forms can sometimes be mistaken for natural animals, but more often they possess fantastic traits — expressions of their companion’s true self — that make them visibly distinct: black feathers that seem to turn to smoke with each movement, a third eye, ghostly lights surrounding the creature… the possibilities are as numerous as the Pneumathoi themselves.


## Growth and Change

A Pneuma does not age physically like ordinary animals, but its appearance may subtly transform over time, reflecting the emotional and spiritual development of its companion. A lightly plumaged juvenile hawk might darken and grow sterner if its companion experiences sorrow or war, while a once fierce Pneuma may soften over the years.

## Communication

Pneumathoi do not use words in the human sense. They communicate through a mixture of sensations, mental imagery, and an emotional echo that only their companion fully understands. Between Aitherion and Pneuma, a fleeting glance can convey warnings, commands, or affection with the precision of speech. In their True Form, some Pneumathoi have demonstrated the ability to project clearer thoughts or even speak in their own voice, though this is rare and usually reserved for exceptionally deep bonds.


## Forms and Potential

Pneumathoi possess multiple forms or states that they may adopt over their lifetimes, and can freely swap between them once they have unlocked each form. All Pneumathoi begin life in their **Basic Form**, which they inhabit for most of their existence. Through introspection and a strong affinity with their companion, higher Forms can be attained, each granting exponentially greater strength and potential.

- **Basic Form** – Small and manageable. Their powers are modest, suited for everyday life or scouting. Many perch on their companion’s shoulder, though some are too large even in this form. Their abilities are comparable to lower-ranked spells.

- **Released Form** – A larger, battle-ready form. Pneumathoi may act as mounts for their companions, and their specific powers become more apparent. Some can unleash sonic attacks, breathe fire, wield magic, heal wounds, or control the weather. Powers in this form are comparable to mid-level magic and spells.

- **Royal Form** – Attainable only by a few individuals who have developed an exceptional bond with their Pneuma. This form symbolises a rare level of communion between companion and Pneuma. In this form, limits to their power are not easily established. The Pneuma expresses a highly evolved form and powers compared to its Released Form. A Pneuma capable of exhaling fire in its Released Form could manifest as pure flames or set the sky itself ablaze. A Pneuma known for brute strength could become a titan capable of rivaling the greatest Titans. A Pneuma capable of healing could maintain and restore the health of all nearby through mere presence. Its powers are on par with the most complex and highest-level magic.

## Relationship with the Aitherion

Within the Aitherion lineage, the Pneuma is considered an extension of family heritage and a symbol of legitimacy.

## Legendary Pneumathoi

Among the ancient records and songs, some Pneumathoi have earned an eternal place in the memory of Oreka:
- Skyfýlax "The Infinite Wing"- A three-tailed silver eagle of colossal size, capable of covering the entire Palace of Ragged Clouds with its wings, who accompanied Alkon the Righteous. It was said that its vocalisations could exert the power to destroy a valley if it so desired, or could be so gentle as to lull a baby to sleep.
- Nyxarion "The Azure Inferno" - A gigantic raven that flew in blue flames. He served Vaedra Aitherion, ruthless strategist, and was last seen flying over the fields of a forgotten land on the night before its destruction.
- Lúmenar "The Grace of Aureon"- A golden heron who accompanied Erian Aitherion, healer and diplomat. It is said that her presence alone calmed all emotions and her song incited peace and reconciliation.

These stories are remembered with pride, although some scholars suggest that the reality of her powers may have been exaggerated over the centuries.
